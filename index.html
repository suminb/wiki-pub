SB Public Wiki¶

Mission Statement¶

Turn wasted research hours into public knowledge.

Rationale¶

Software engineers, including myself, spend a non-trivial amount of time for going through various resources online (i.e., Google, StackOverflow, etc.) to search for technical information and knowledge. If we are lucky, we find information we need without much struggle by scrambling few keywords. However, if we are unlucky, in which we are in many cases, we end up digging through a load of documentations and mailing lists like a detective chasing after a crime suspect.

This process inheritently gives us great opportunities to expand our horizon, discovering alternatives of our current technical decisions, less-known features of a particular technology, and other interesting stuff. Over time, we tend to forget what we’ve learned in the process, especially when they are irralavant to the problem at hand.

Even if we are dealing with relavant materials, it is difficult to remember all the gory details like parameter names, option names in a configuration file, environment variables, and so on and so forth. This brings us back to where we started. We find ourselves in a situation where we dig into the matters that we have seen at some point in the past.

What if we took some notes about our findings? What if we at least saved the URLs of the materials we found with some keywords or perhaps with a brief description? We could have saved a lot of time for ourselves. Or, even better, we could have saved someone else’s time and effort. The would could have been a better place.

This project has started in an attempt to salvage some of those wasted research hours by turning them into public knowledge.
